Representation and 'true' angles
--------------------------------

Angles are usually represented in degrees as 0-360 or +/-180.
And by adding (or subtracting) multiples of 360 you can generated infinite apparent equivalents for any given angle.

But they are only synonyms from a presentation or resultant point of view - actual angles beyond 360 represent winding or turns.
So 360 is one turn, 720 is two turns etc and the distinction is important in many sports, skateboarding for example.

The turtle's direction (or heading) is a true angle and needs to preserve winding.

So I need to make sure I have a clear separation between:
* true angles
* apparent or presentational angles

And that the correct maths is applied in the correct spots.

Some of this will vary on things like
* How the space is configured - clockwise/ccw
* The needs of the particular calculation
* User/UI preferences
